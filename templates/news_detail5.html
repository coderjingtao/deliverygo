What Is a Power assisted bicycle

In Victoria, a power assisted bicycle is:

1.A pedal cycle with one or more auxiliary propulsion motors attached which has a combined maximum power output not exceeding 200 watts.

2.A bicycle certified as a Pedalec. This bicycle features an auxiliary power producing no more than 250 watts and specifies this as a continuous rating. It also restricts the top power assisted speed to 25 kilometres per hour and requires the rider to pedal to access the power

Please Note:

Power assisted bicycles are not required to be registered nor the rider required to be licensed. Standard bicycle road rules apply to power-assisted bicycles. Please not that if your power assisted bicycle is not followed the above definition, it probably is a motorised bicycle and you are required to register it and hold motor license. Different road rules will be applied for motorised bicycle
