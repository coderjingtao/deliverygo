Regulation Information

 

Road rules

  • Bicycle lanes

    • If there’s a bicycle lane on the road heading in the same direction as you, you must use this when riding a bike (unless it’s not practical to do so)

  • Riding with other riders

    • A cyclist can ride next to another cyclist (this is called ‘riding two abreast’) on the road with the following rules:

    1. On single-lane roads, cyclists can’t ride beside more than one other cyclist (unless overtaking).
    2. On multi-lane roads, cyclists:
    3. can’t ride more than two abreast in any single marked lane (unless overtaking)
    4. can ride more than two abreast across multiple lanes.
    5. When travelling two abreast, cyclists shouldn’t be more than 1.5 metres apart.
  • Riding on the footpath

    • Adults are allowed to ride on the footpath only if accompanying a bike rider under the age of 12, or if suffering a disability and carrying a relevant medical certificate or otherwise exempted. Bike riders must always give way to pedestrians on footpaths and shared paths.

Visit VicRoads for more information.
